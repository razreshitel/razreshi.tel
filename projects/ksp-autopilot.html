PROJECT · ksp-autopilot

Autonomous rockets in Kerbal Space Program

Python autopilot scripts that fly rockets in Kerbal Space Program with no human input, driven live over the kRPC mod - telemetry out, commands streams in, over TCP. Two scripts do the work: one launches a vessel to a permanent, stable orbit; the other flies a booster back to a soft upright landing near the pad while the upper stage reaches orbit on the same flight. Proven in-game across 25 autonomous flights. A hobby project, not a product.

Python kRPC guidance & control PID · vis-viva no computer vision sensor-only

What it does

02 scripts
01 Orbit

Launch to a permanent orbit

launch_to_orbit.py flies the active vessel up on a gravity-turn ascent and circularizes at apoapsis into a stable orbit that will not decay - 70-80 km. It handles single-stage or two-stage rockets: a two-stage craft auto-stages, dropping the first stage on its own once its fuel hits zero and finishing the orbit on the next.

Target altitude, gravity-turn end, the first-stage fuel reserve, and an optional physics time-warp to run the ascent faster are all tunable from the command line.

02 Recover

Launch and fly the booster back

launch_and_recover.py runs a Falcon-9-style profile on a two-stage rocket: launch, then separate the first stage once it burns down to a set fuel limit (30% by default). From there both stages fly at once in one control loop - the booster turns around, burns back toward the pad, deploys airbrakes on the descent, and brakes to a soft upright touchdown, while the second stage carries on to orbit.

Best in-game run: the booster came down alive - 0.3° off vertical, undamaged, −2.85 m/s at touchdown - with the upper stage in a stable 70.5 × 84 km orbit on the same flight.

The constraint

sensor-only

No cheating - sensor-only flight

The autopilot reads only what a real flight computer could, then works the rest out itself: orbital elements, time to apoapsis, the suicide-burn, and a drag-aware prediction of where the booster will fall - the way real avionics would.

Onboard, like a real rocket

  • position & velocity (GPS / INS)
  • altitude & radar altitude
  • vertical speed & speed
  • mass + thrust + remaining fuel
  • attitude
  • an onboard atmosphere / drag model

Never - the game's god view

  • map-view apoapsis / periapsis
  • the game's predicted impact point
  • maneuver-node Δv
  • anything a real booster can't sense
